# Corporate Trade & Treasury Product Parameters

## 1. Letter of Credit (LC)

Companies that regularly engage in international trade activities, whether import or export, with material transaction values and dependency on overseas trading partners, have a need for payment risk mitigation instruments. This need becomes more relevant if the company operates in the manufacturing or trading sector, uses imported raw materials, or targets export market expansion, thereby requiring payment certainty and trust between counterparties.

## 2. Supply Chain Finance (SCF)

Companies that regularly engage in international trade activities, whether import or export, with material transaction values and dependency on overseas trading partners, have a need for payment risk mitigation instruments. This need becomes more relevant if the company operates in the manufacturing or trading sector, uses imported raw materials, or targets export market expansion, thereby requiring payment certainty and trust between counterparties. Companies that play an important role within a supply chain—either as an anchor buyer or as a supplier to large corporations—and maintain recurring invoice flows as well as long-term business relationships with partners, may require working capital optimization solutions. This need increases when there is a significant gap between payment and receivable tenors, or when the company has many small-scale business partners that are highly dependent on stable cash flow.
